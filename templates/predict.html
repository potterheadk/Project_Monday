Home Predictor Helper Insights
  • The GRE is a standardized test required for admission to many graduate programs worldwide.
  • It assesses verbal reasoning, quantitative reasoning, analytical writing, and critical thinking skills.
  • Scores are used by admissions committees to evaluate applicants' readiness for graduate-level academic work.
  • The test is administered by the Educational Testing Service (ETS) and can be taken at designated test centers worldwide.
  • The TOEFL is a standardized test for non-native English speakers to demonstrate proficiency in English.
  • It's commonly required for admission to English-speaking universities and colleges.
  • The test assesses reading, listening, speaking, and writing skills in English.
  • Like the GRE, TOEFL scores are used by admissions committees to evaluate applicants' language abilities.
  • A Statement of Purpose is a written essay that accompanies a graduate school application.
  • It outlines an applicant's academic background, career goals, reasons for pursuing a particular program, and relevant experiences.
  • The SOP is an opportunity for applicants to showcase their motivation, qualifications, and fit for the program they're applying to.
  • It's important for applicants to be clear, concise, and compelling in their statements.
  • A Letter of Recommendation is a document written by someone who knows the applicant well academically or professionally.
  • It provides insights into the applicant's character, skills, achievements, and potential for success in graduate studies.
  • Typically, letters are written by professors, employers, or mentors who can speak to the applicant's abilities and qualifications.
  • Strong letters of recommendation can greatly enhance an applicant's chances of admission, as they offer additional perspectives on the applicant beyond what's presented in their application materials.
